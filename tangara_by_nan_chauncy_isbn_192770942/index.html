Tangara by Nan Chauncy

Title: Tangara
Author: Nan Chauncy

Review: Tangara, written by Nan Chauncy, is a delightful children’s book that takes readers on a magical journey through the eyes of a young boy named Tangara. The story is set in the picturesque Tasmanian wilderness and is filled with adventure, friendship, and the power of believing in oneself.

The book is primarily targeted at children between the ages of 8 and 12, although readers of all ages can enjoy and appreciate the beautifully crafted story. The language used is simple yet engaging, making it easy for young readers to follow and understand.

Throughout the book, several life lessons are woven into the narrative. These include the importance of friendship, trust, perseverance, and self-confidence. The story also highlights the value of preserving nature and respecting the environment.

Overall, Tangara is a charming and captivating book that will leave a lasting impression on its readers. The vivid descriptions of the Tasmanian wilderness and the engaging characters make it a must-read for children and adults alike. I highly recommend adding this book to your collection.
