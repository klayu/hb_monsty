Noughts and Crosses

Title: Noughts and Crosses
Author: Malorie Blackman
Target Age Group: 10-14 years old

Noughts and Crosses is a captivating novel by Malorie Blackman that takes readers on a thrilling journey through a world where racism and prejudice are reversed. The story follows the lives of two teenagers, Callum and Sephy, who come from different backgrounds and are faced with challenges in their friendship as they grow up in a society divided by race.

The book targets children between the ages of 10 and 14 years old, as it contains themes that are relatable and thought-provoking for this age group. The life lessons covered in the book include the importance of friendship, understanding, empathy, and standing up against injustice.

The story is engaging and well-written, with relatable characters that readers can connect with on a personal level. The plot is fast-paced and keeps the reader’s interest throughout, making it an enjoyable read for both children and adults.

I would highly recommend Noughts and Crosses to anyone looking for a thought-provoking and engaging read. The book serves as a reminder of the importance of empathy, understanding, and standing up against injustice, and it is a must-read for children and adults alike.
