Troy by Adele Geras

Title: Troy
Author: Adele Geras

Review: Troy is a beautifully written children’s book by Adele Geras, which tells the story of a young girl named Troy who lives in a small village. The book is suitable for children aged 8-12 years old and is a perfect blend of adventure, friendship, and self-discovery.

The story follows Troy as she embarks on a journey to find her missing father, who has been captured by the Greek army. Along the way, she encounters various challenges and learns valuable life lessons, such as the importance of courage, determination, and the power of friendship.

One of the most remarkable aspects of this book is the vivid and engaging narrative that transports readers to the world of ancient Greece. The characters are well-developed, and the plot is both exciting and emotionally engaging.

In addition to its captivating storyline, Troy also teaches valuable life lessons, such as the importance of perseverance, teamwork, and believing in oneself. These lessons are subtly woven into the story, making it an excellent choice for parents and educators looking to instill positive values in young readers.

Overall, Troy is a must-read for children aged 8-12 years old. The book is a perfect blend of adventure, history, and life lessons, making it an enriching and enjoyable experience for young readers. I highly recommend adding Troy to your child’s library and embarking on this exciting journey with them.
